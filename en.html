About

I'm currently teaching my first workshop on Video Game Design and Development. I'm an educator and I've been working in IT for the past 10 years. I had the opportunity to teach courses and workshops on areas such as PC Repair, Networks and Web Development and Design

Experience in Video Games

I’m self-taught and have one year of hands-on experience in Game Design and Development, working both in teams and independently. I’ve worked on several playable projects where I handled everything from development and designing core mechanics to visual and narrative design. These games were created under tight deadlines and are intended as prototypes. Each one explores a game mechanic or idea, which I use to test design concepts and improve my development process. While they are simple, they represent the transformation of ideas into playable experiences, as well as demonstrating scope and time management.

Approach

When it comes to teaching I look into combining theory with critical thinking and practice, as a way to learn by doing. I introduce key concepts and then guide students with questions to help them apply those ideas to real game examples. Each class includes assignments that reinforce hands-on practice while also encouraging research and exploration of topics beyond the course content. In my experience, this approach helps students build confidence and independence in their field of study.

Projects

Video Game Design and Development Workshop

The workshop is part of a programme of the government's initiative on technological skills and it focuses in teaching the principles of game design and development.

The goal is for students to leave with a platformer game, as well as their own design ideas and the knowledge to begin their studies in game design and/or development.

The key skills taught include:

  • Design principles.
  • Rapid prototyping.
  • Design analysis.
  • Use of Godot Engine.
  • Scripting with GDScript.
  • Version control.
  • Publishing games on online stores.
